The Weight of Force: Seattle Police Incidents Across Race and Time

Andy Kim & Ryan Kim

Credit: The Weight of Force: Seattle Police Incidents Across Race and Time by Andy Kim

The question we were trying to answer with our visualization is "Does the different level of police use of force in Seattle fall disproportionately on certain racial groups, and how had that distribution shifted across 2014-2022?"

At first we thought of using stacked area chart where the area would show how much crime was committed by each racial group during that year, but we also wanted to show the intensity of police force used, so we thought using two separated bar chart was more suitable when we decided to compare both the intensity of police force used in incidents separated by race and total crime rate also separated by race. So, we used two bar charts where one shows the intensity of police force used for the crimes separated by race and the other shows the total crime rate separated by race. The visualization on the right shows the total crime rate chart separated by race. For this chart we decided not to use color encoding since the racial groups were already labeled in the x-axis and color encoding them would cause redundancy in information. The visualization on the left shows the intensity of the police force used for crimes separated by racial groups. We decided to use color encoding for visualization since adding the three hue sequential scale will reinforce the severity ordering. We grouped the data into racial groupd and each group has three bars where each bar represents each force level. This shows how severe the force was within each race and can see what racial group commits the most intense crimes which results in higher intensity of police force used. In both visualizations we made the LabelAngle in the x-axis to be -30 degrees rather than having to read the label completely sideways so that it is easier for the audience to read the labels.

There are three interaction designs we incorporated into our visualization, scroll, play button, and tooltip on hover.
